Frame this as a **temporary special event** — the same category Cirque du Soleil uses
for their Big Top tent in MOA's north parking lot. This avoids permanent construction
permits, zoning changes, and the multi-year regulatory path of a fixed transit system.

- This is the exact model: temporary structure, parking lot, ticketed attraction, limited run

### 3. State of Minnesota — Amusement Ride Regulation

**Governing statute:** [Minnesota Statute 184B — Regulation of Amusement Rides](https://www.revisor.mn.gov/statutes/cite/184B/full)

**Administering agency:** Minnesota Department of Labor and Industry

**Key question: Is a JPods demo an "amusement ride" under 184B?**

**Definition (184B.01):** "Amusement ride" means a mechanical device that carries or
conveys passengers along, around, or over a fixed or restricted route or course for
the purpose of giving its passengers amusement, pleasure, thrills, or excitement.

**JPods position:** JPods is a transportation system, not an amusement ride. However,
a 2-month demo in a parking lot — where riders board for the experience, not to travel
to a destination — will almost certainly be classified as an amusement ride for
regulatory purposes. **Do not fight this classification.** Embrace it. ASTM F24
compliance is something we want anyway, and it gives us credibility. We need both
government coercion to enforce safety and liberty to allow innovation — ASTM F24
provides both (insurance, inspectors, tort law enforce accountability; no federal
permission needed to innovate).

**Mike Teske handles this.** He has certified 110 major rides for Disney and Universal
Studios. He knows the ASTM F24 process, the inspectors, the documentation. This is
not a research problem — it's an execution problem with an experienced hand on it.

**If classified as amusement ride, 184B requires:**

| Requirement | Detail | Status |
|-------------|--------|--------|
| **Insurance** | $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate liability | Need to obtain |
| **Annual inspection** | By NAARSO/AIMS Level II or III certified inspector | Mike Teske coordinates |
| **Inspection affidavit** | Filed with commissioner within 10 days of inspection | After inspection |
| **Daily inspections** | Per ASTM F 770-93 before each day of operation | Nora + operator |
| **Operator training** | Documented training policy — procedures, safety, evacuation | Need to develop |
| **Incident reporting** | Report serious injuries to state/local law enforcement | Protocol needed |
| **3-year records** | Daily inspection records maintained 3 years minimum | Alice tracks |

**ASTM standards that apply:**
- **ASTM F 846-92** — Inspection standard (annual)
- **ASTM F 893-04** — Inspection standard (annual)
- **ASTM F 770-93** — Daily inspection standard
- **ASTM F2960** — Standard Practice for Permanent Amusement Railway Ride Tracks
  (closest to JPods guideway — may apply or may need variance)
- **ASTM F24 Committee** — 29 standards in Volume 15.07; identify which apply to
  guideway-based passenger conveyance

### 4. State of Minnesota — Carnival & Circus Electrical Permit

**Separate from 184B.** The MN Dept of Labor and Industry has a dedicated carnival/circus
electrical permit track that applies to any temporary event with powered rides.

**Administering agency:** MN Dept of Labor and Industry, Electrical Unit
- Email: dli.electricity@state.mn.us
- Phone: (651) 284-5005

**Process:**
1. Create an account at the [online electrical permit portal](https://www.dli.mn.gov/business/electrical-contractors/electrical-permits-contractors)
2. Submit a **Transitory Event Operator (TEO)** account request form — email to dli.electricity@state.mn.us
3. Wait for TEO account confirmation before applying
4. Submit carnival/circus electrical permit application through portal
5. Provide transitory operation event schedule and list of transitory assets

**Key documents (downloaded as PDFs):**
- [Carnival and Circus Permit Instructions (PDF, updated 2026-04-01)](https://www.dli.mn.gov/sites/default/files/pdf/eli-guide-carnival-and-circus.pdf) — step-by-step application guide
- [Affidavit of Amusement Ride Inspection (PDF)](https://www.dli.mn.gov/sites/default/files/pdf/eli_carnival_affidavit.pdf) — required inspection form
- [Portable and Temporary Power overview](https://www.dli.mn.gov/portable-power)

**Why this matters for JPods:** The TEO account and electrical permit are required for
any temporary powered ride installation. This is the same path every mobile carnival
uses — well-trodden, predictable, and independent of whether the city classifies
JPods as a "special event" or "carnival." Get the TEO account started early.

### 5. Hennepin County Carnival Permit Precedent — Minneapolis

Minneapolis (largest city in Hennepin County) has the most detailed public carnival
permit framework. This is useful as a reference even though Bloomington has its own
licensing — the state requirements are identical.

**Minneapolis carnival license fees (2026):**

| Category | First Day | Each Additional Day |
|----------|-----------|---------------------|
| 1–9 attractions | $163 | $53 |
| 10–19 attractions | $257 | $53 |
| 20+ attractions | $515 | $53 |

**Contact:** 612-673-2080, Public Service Building, 505 Fourth Ave S, Room 220

**Fee schedule source:** [2026 Proposed License Fee Schedule (PDF)](https://lims.minneapolismn.gov/Download/RCAV2/50978/Proposed-2026-License-Fee-Schedule.pdf)

**JPods demo would likely fall in the 1–9 category** (2–3 stations + guideway = 1
attraction with multiple components). At $163 first day + $53/day × ~60 days = ~$3,300
for a 2-month Minneapolis carnival license. Bloomington fees may differ but this
establishes the order of magnitude — carnival licensing is cheap.

**MN Rule 8130.0900 (Sales Tax):** Carnival ride gross receipts are fully taxable,
including lump-sum fees paid by sponsors when patrons ride free. If MOA sponsors free
rides for visitors, the sponsorship amount is still taxable.

## Engineering Deliverables (from Gordy)

The permit package requires professional engineering documentation. These are
Gordy's identified deliverables:

### 1. Quality Program
A documented quality program covering fabrication, installation, inspection,
and operational quality assurance for the demo system.

### 2. Overview Drawing — Demo JPod System at MOA Parking Lot
Site plan showing the complete demo system layout on the Mall of America parking
lot — stations, guideway alignment, access points, fencing, operator station,
electrical connections, and relationship to adjacent structures (IKEA, mall entries).

### 3. Support Pole and Foundation Drawing
Engineering drawing of the guideway support pole and its foundation.

**Key design decision — freestanding baseplate vs. buried foundation:**
A temporary installation favors a **freestanding broad baseplate anchored to
pavement** rather than a buried foundation. The baseplate is bolted/anchored to
the parking lot surface to prevent sliding. This avoids excavation, soil testing
costs, and pavement restoration at teardown.

Drawing must include disclaimer: *"Design to be modified based upon results of
soil tests"* (even with baseplate option — covers the case where pavement bearing
capacity requires verification).

### 4. JPod Vehicle Sketch
Engineering sketch of the JPod vehicle showing dimensions, passenger capacity,
safety features (restraints, emergency stop), and connection to guideway.

### 5. Applicable Codes List

| Code | Edition | What it governs |
|------|---------|----------------|
| **Minnesota Building Code** | 2020 | Temporary structures, foundations, occupancy |
| **Minnesota Fire Code** | 2020 | Fire safety, egress, emergency access |
| **Minnesota Electrical Code** | Current | All electrical — solar, grid backup, vehicle charging |
| **ASCE/SEI 7-22** | 2022 | Minimum Design Loads and Associated Criteria for Buildings and Other Structures — wind, snow, seismic, live loads on guideway |
| **ASTM F 846-92** | — | Amusement ride inspection (annual) |
| **ASTM F 893-04** | — | Amusement ride inspection (annual) |
| **ASTM F 770-93** | — | Amusement ride daily inspection |
| **ASTM F2960** | — | Permanent Amusement Railway Ride Tracks (closest standard to JPods guideway) |
| **MN Statute 184B** | Current | Regulation of Amusement Rides — insurance, inspection, reporting |

**ASCE/SEI 7-22 is critical for the guideway.** It governs wind loads (September–October
in Minnesota can see 60+ mph gusts), snow loads (unlikely in Sep–Oct but required for
code compliance), and the live load of pods + passengers on the guideway structure.
The support pole and baseplate design must meet ASCE 7-22 load combinations.

## Demo Design — What Noelle Needs to Validate

**Minimum viable demo network:**
- 2 stations (departure + arrival) — or 3 for a loop
- Guideway length: 200–500m (enough to demonstrate speed and quiet operation)
- Elevation: guideway posts in parking lot surface (temporary footings, not permanent)
- Power: solar panels on guideway + temporary grid connection as backup
- Vehicles: 2–4 pods (scale model or full prototype depending on readiness)

**Foundation approach (Gordy):** Freestanding broad baseplate anchored to pavement.
No excavation. Bolted to parking surface to prevent sliding. Avoids soil testing
and pavement restoration at teardown.

**Noelle's Event Station rule applies:**
- Burst demand pattern (event visitors, not commuters)
- One-sided access acceptable for demo
- More slots during peak hours (Sally manages queue)

**Safety envelope:**
- Speed limit for demo: recommend 15 km/h (walking speed) for initial approval
- Emergency stop accessible from ground level
- Operator station with full visibility
- Weather protocol (wind speed limits per ASCE/SEI 7-22 for elevated guideway)
