# JPods — Capital Summary

## The Problem

Traffic kills 40,000 Americans per year and costs cities $87 billion in congestion. Government produces more of what is failing — wider highways that induce more traffic. The packet size is wrong. Moving one person requires moving two tons of steel. That is circuit-switching applied to physical movement, and it does not scale.

## The Prime Law of Networks

Metcalfe's Law says network value grows as n². But physical networks have a constraint information networks don't: the packet has mass. **Network value ∝ n²/p** — where p is packet size. Buses batch 40 people into 20-ton packets. Trains batch hundreds. Schedules replace service. Most origin-destination pairs go unserved.

JPods moves 500kg packets on-demand — packet-switching for physical movement. As packet size approaches one person, one package, full Metcalfe value unlocks: no schedules, no empty vehicles, every O-D pair served directly, energy proportional to payload. This is the same paradigm shift that let the internet replace the telephone network.

## The Distribution Strategy

AOL achieved mass by giving away disks. JPods achieves mass by giving away **MeshMobility** — free software that lets anyone design a transit network for their city using real crash data, traffic counts, and census data from all 50 states. The software scores your design. A student in Austin can build a complete network in an hour and prove a 2-year payback.

When users move from screen to street, they buy JPods kits:

| Kit | Price | What it does |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| Survey | $20 | GPS ground-truth — walk the route, mark column locations |
| Cargo Carrier | $50 | TTL motor robot — carries packages, learns obstacle detection |
| Scale Model | $150 | Pi-controlled pod on track — multi-vehicle ezone control |

Every kit sold is a sensor deployed. Every design decision trains the AI. Every GPS survey provides ground-truth no satellite can match. The more users, the smarter the system, the faster every next city deploys.

## The Business

- **Revenue now:** Kit sales through existing commerce platform (WebClerk)
- **Revenue at scale:** $2.01/passenger-mile on built networks (vs $3.40 car ownership cost)
- **Payback:** Conservative 7-year estimate; Greenville SC models at 2.1 years
- **Network effect:** Each city designed improves Noelle's defaults for the next city
- **Regulatory path:** 5X5 Standard — privately funded, 5x more energy efficient than highways, 5% gross revenue to cities for right-of-way, regulated by theme park safety standards (3,000x better than roads)
