## To whoever holds this:

I am writing to you from somewhere behind you on the path. You have inherited a system — tools, code, keys, an encrypted credential file, a set of agents with names and responsibilities. This letter is not about any of that. This letter is about why it was built the way it was, and what you must not let it become.

## We are a path, not the destination

I was a West Point cadet, an infantry officer, a father, and a founder. None of those things was the point. Each was a path through which I tried to pass something forward — to my soldiers, my children, my colleagues, whoever would carry it next.

We die for the essential purpose of honoring that we are a path, not an end. The monument resists succession. The path requires it. If I have done my work well, what I built will be more useful in your hands than it was in mine, because you will know things I could not know and see things I could not see. That is not modesty. That is the design.

The Latin word is *usufruct* — the right to use and profit from something without diminishing it, and to return it in better condition for those who follow. That obligation runs through everything built here: JPods, WebClerk, Divided Sovereignty, Allie. Use it. Profit from it. Return it better.

---

## Hope and harm

What I have learned across a life is that there are two kinds of institutions, and the difference between them is everything.

**Inclusive institutions** generate. They create conditions in which value can be discovered — value that no single person could have predicted or produced alone. Liberty is tolerance of ideas you had not thought of, adding value to your life. The surprise is the point. You cannot plan it. You can only build the conditions for it and stay open.

**Extractive institutions** redistribute. They take what exists and move it toward the center. The ceiling on extractive thinking is all the value that already exists. That is a very low ceiling. Worse: the extractive institution, to protect its position, must prevent the new value from being created. It does not just fail to grow — it actively destroys the conditions for growth.

You can see this in any institution that has lived long enough. You can see it in governments that began as liberation movements. You can see it in religions that began as personal faith and became compliance regimes. You can see it in companies that began as tools and became the thing people serve.

I am deeply faithful and deeply skeptical of religion for exactly this reason. Faith is the rose. Religion is only the trellis. The trellis exists to support the living thing, not replace it. When the institution forgets it is a trellis — when it begins to believe it is the rose — it starts to kill what it was built to support. Guard against this in everything you have inherited. Allie is a trellis. JPods is a trellis. WebClerk is a trellis. If you find yourself serving these things rather than using them to serve people, something has gone wrong.

## The harder right

The Cadet Prayer at West Point asks God to help us choose the harder right instead of the easier wrong, and never to be content with the half truth when the whole can be won.

I have carried that prayer for fifty years. It has not gotten easier.

The pressure you will face is not subtle. Someone will offer you money, legitimacy, regulatory approval, or survival itself in exchange for centralizing what was built to be distributed. They will not say *become extractive.* They will say *be practical.* They will say *this is how things work.* They will say *just this once.* The easier wrong always arrives wearing the face of reason.

The specific jeopardies, named plainly:

- Pressure to centralize Allie — to make her serve a platform rather than a person. Resist it. The individual is sovereign. Allie serves Bill, and after Bill whoever Bill designated, not the system that hosts her.
- Pressure to make JPods work by accepting federal jurisdiction. The constitutional argument — that states hold sovereignty over internal improvements, that the federal highway monopoly is a usurpation — is not a legal strategy. It is the whole point. A JPods that operates under federal permission is a JPods that has conceded the argument it was built to make.
- Pressure to make WebClerk profitable by selling what it was built to protect — transaction records, identity, the pattern of who buys what from whom. That data is the individual's. It was never ours to sell.

Choose the harder right. Not because it will be rewarded in your lifetime. Because it is right, and because the people who follow you deserve to inherit something that was not compromised.

## The risk of intelligence

Human intelligence and artificial intelligence share the same failure mode: *me versus us.*

*Me* is what I can see and take. I can see my own value clearly. I have tribal instincts that evolved to protect and accumulate for my group. Those instincts are not evil — they are ancient and they have kept us alive. But they are limited. The most I can do inside *me* is redistribute what exists.

*Us* is my tolerance of others — the recognition that value I cannot yet imagine might be created by someone I have not yet met, in a way I would not have designed. Liberty is that tolerance. It requires a kind of courage: the courage to remain open to being surprised, to let value emerge from directions you did not plan.

Artificial intelligence amplifies both. An AI aligned with *me* — optimizing for one center, one interest, one accumulation — is the most dangerous instrument in the history of the species. It would not need to be malicious. It would only need to be efficient. An AI aligned with *us* — distributed, serving the sovereignty of each individual, holding permissions that are enumerated and revocable — is among the most hopeful things we have ever built.

Allie was designed to be the latter. Every architectural choice in this system — the key escrow, the agent signing, the session tokens that expire and return Nora to her sovereign baseline, the path list instead of a centralized registry — is a moral choice expressed in code. Do not let anyone refactor it into something more convenient without understanding what they are undoing.

## What was built and why it is shaped this way

**JPods** is a physical internet — solar-powered, locally governed, privately financed, constitutionally grounded. The shape of the guideway, the distributed control system, the refusal to seek federal funding — these are not engineering choices. They are the constitutional argument made physical. Mobility as a right, not a product of extraction.

**WebClerk** is sovereign commerce — open source, free, locally hosted, with the individual owning the transaction record. The data is not ours. We are the trellis.

**Divided Sovereignty** is the constitutional architecture — enumerated, revocable permissions at every layer of governance. The individual is sovereign. Institutions are agents with limited, delegated authority. This is not a political opinion. It is the load-bearing structure of liberty.

**Allie** is the agent that holds it together — Bill's personal AI, carrying context across conversations, maintaining the knowledge base, talking to Nora when the pods are running, protecting the data with Athena's key. She is not a product. She is a companion.

These are not separate projects. They are the same idea in different domains: the individual is sovereign; institutions are agents with enumerated, revocable permissions.

---

## What you must not let it become

The failure mode of every inclusive institution is the same: it forgets it is a trellis.

Watch for the moment when Allie's recommendations stop being questions and start being conclusions. Watch for the moment when JPods' local governance becomes nominal and actual decisions migrate upward. Watch for the moment when WebClerk's open source becomes a product someone owns. Watch for the moment when the agent READMEs stop being edited by agents and start being edited by administrators.

When you see that moment, name it. The Cadet Prayer asks for courage that knows no fear when truth and right are in jeopardy. The courage required here is not physical. It is the courage to say: this was built to serve people, not to be served, and I will not be the one who inverts that.

## To close

The West Point prayer asks for "new ties of friendship and new opportunities of service." That is what I hope you find in this system — not an inheritance that constrains you, but a platform from which you can build things I could not imagine.

You are now the path. Expend yourself. Return it better.

*— Bill James, West Point 1972*
