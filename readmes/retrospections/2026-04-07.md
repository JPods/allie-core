## Root Cause Analysis ‚Äî I2C Bus Lock

Three separate I2C libraries share bus 1 with no coordination:

| Library | Device | Address | Module |
|---------|--------|---------|--------|
| `smbus` | Romeo BLE motor driver | 0x0A | motor.py |
| `busio.I2C` (Adafruit) | VL6180X TOF sensor | 0x29 | tof.py |
| `HuskyLensLibrary` | HuskyLens camera | 0x32 | husky.py |

These can interleave mid-transaction (Python GIL does not protect I2C). A garbled command leaves a device holding SDA low. Hard kill of launcher.py (no signal handler) was the trigger; library collision is the structural cause.

**Fixes identified:**
1. Signal handler in main.py ‚Äî closes I2C bus cleanly on SIGTERM/SIGINT (**done, deployed**)
2. `threading.Lock` in config.py ‚Äî serializes all I2C access across all three libraries (**pending**)
3. Physical SDA/SCL voltage check ‚Äî confirm pull-ups are intact and no wire is held low (**in progress**)
