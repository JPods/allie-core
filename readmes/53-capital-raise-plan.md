## Step 1: Friends & Family — $240,000

**Purpose:** Start building at Mall of America. Launch 10xMakers.com distribution. Raise $6M to complete MOA.

**What $240K buys:**
- Mall of America demonstration network — first public-facing JPods installation
- 10xMakers.com platform launch — free MeshMobility software, student kit store, leaderboard
- Sign "We Win, You Win" teaming agreements with vendors to assemble the $6M in committed capability

**How $240K becomes $6M — "We Win, You Win" Teaming Agreements:**

JPods doesn't raise $6M in cash. Vendors co-invest. Under proven teaming agreements, each vendor invests their own capital to prepare products and capabilities for the network. JPods commits to purchase at 25x their investment when the network is built — or on any future JPods network. Neither party is liable if the network is never built. The commitment never terminates.

This aligns incentives: vendors bet on their own capabilities, not on JPods' fundraising. A steel fabricator invests $50K in guideway tooling → JPods commits $1.25M in orders. A motor vendor invests $20K in vehicle drive systems → JPods commits $500K. The $240K F&F round funds JPods' side of the table — technology, compliance, coordination — while vendors assemble the construction capability at their own risk.

Precedent: JPods has executed this structure with Stadia Construction (Georgia) and Texas Mobility Company LLC. The template is proven and ready to replicate with Minnesota vendors for MOA.

**Why Minnesota:** JPods has an existing advantage in Minnesota. SkyRide Technology ([skyridetechnology.com](https://skyridetechnology.com/)) has an operational guideway in Waconia, MN. JPods has a contract providing SkyRide with robots — people can ride the network *now*, before a single dollar is spent on new construction. The pedal-powered version of SkyRide is installed on 3 Carnival Cruise Ships and was rated **Best Cruise Ship Feature of 2016 and 2017** — millions of passengers have already experienced the guideway form factor. ([library.jpods.com/skyride](https://library.jpods.com/skyride/))

**Why MOA:** 42 million visitors per year. The demonstration sells itself. Every visitor sees solar-powered pod transit working — not a video, not a simulation, a ride. MOA is the world's largest proof-of-concept venue. And the Waconia guideway is 35 miles away — investors can ride SkyRide in the morning and see the MOA plans in the afternoon.

**Terms for F&F lenders:**
- Repayment: 18 months or upon closing the $20M raise (whichever comes first)
- Equity bonus: shares equal to **2x the loan value** granted at closing
- Conversion option: lender may convert loan to equity at **3x the loan value**
- Example: $20,000 loan → repaid $20,000 + granted $40,000 in shares. *Option:* convert $20,000 loan to $60,000 of additional equity

**Why these terms are generous:** Early capital carries the most risk and deserves the most reward. The 2x bonus and 3x conversion reflect that the F&F round makes everything else possible. Without this round, there is no MOA, no 10xMakers, no $6M raise.

## Step 3: Expand — $600M+ (DOE Loan Program Office)

**Purpose:** Full-scale national deployment.

**Prerequisite:** Technical Readiness Level 7 (achieved through MOA + first city deployments in Step 2).

**DOE Loan Program Office:** Previous discussions regarding $600M to support full-scale deployment. The DOE LPO has funded $40B+ in energy infrastructure projects. JPods qualifies under energy self-reliance (solar-powered networks collecting 2+ MWh per network mile per day).

**What $600M buys:**
- Simultaneous deployment in 10+ cities
- Manufacturing at scale — kits, guideway components, vehicles
- 50-state 10xMakers presence — student networks in every state
- Network effects compound — Noelle gets smarter with every city, every deployment costs less

**The railroad precedent:** Scaling will replay the deployment of railroads and streetcar networks. The Pacific Railroad Act of 1862 didn't break the barrier — that took the corrected 1864 Act. Four years later, 1,911 miles crossed the nation. 260,000 miles were built in 30 years. By 1916, every US city over 10,000 people had a privately funded streetcar network.

Rail networks are "digital" — the network manages the packets. This is the same architecture JPods applies. The Congressional OTA study (PB-244854) identified Automated Guideway Transit as the solution to the 1973 Oil Embargo, noting that Federal "institutional failures" had blocked guideway innovation for "four to six decades." Those institutional failures trace to unconstitutional Federal mercantile monopolies: the Federal-Aid Highway Act of 1916 replaced 45% of 470 ton-mpg railroads with 25 mpg roads. Streetcars were wiped out. The Federal communications monopoly (1918) mandated rotary telephones for 64 years.

Once the barrier breaks, deployment is explosive. Railroads proved it. The Internet proved it. JPods is next. (Full analysis: *Climate Change Root Cause* — available with this packet.)

## The Determined Campaign — Following the Internet's Path

The Internet didn't appear overnight. It incrementally built an ecology — from the first video call in 1927 to the Mother of All Demos in 1968 to TCP/IP in 1983. Courts declaring the Federal communications monopoly unconstitutional in 1982 broke the mercantile regulatory barriers. AOL's 1993 "You've got mail" triggered viral adoption. Each step was necessary. None was sufficient alone.

JPods has applied these incremental steps into a determined campaign:

| Internet | JPods | What it accomplished |
|----------|-------|---------------------|
| 1927 first video call | 2002 Patent 6,810,817 filed | Technology proven |
| 1968 Mother of All Demos | 2011 TEDxAtlanta | Vision demonstrated publicly |
| 1979 pirate touch-tone phones | 2014 Secaucus Ordinance (unanimous) | Pirates break through at the edge |
| 1982 courts rule monopoly unconstitutional | 2021 MA H5248 funded unanimously | Regulatory barrier broken |
| 1983 TCP/IP on ARPANET | 2025 5X5 Standard + GSP franchise | Commercial standard established |

We are at the AOL moment. The ecology is built. The regulatory barrier is broken. The commercial standard exists. What triggers viral adoption is mass distribution of free software and low-cost kits that let anyone design, survey, build, and ride their own transit network.
