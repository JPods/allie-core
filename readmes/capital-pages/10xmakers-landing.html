Profit by Cutting Costs 10x

80% of trips are under 10 miles. JPods at $0.03/passenger-mile vs $0.38 for cars. MassDOT cost data, US DOE energy data.

Why This Matters

Highway central planning of the economy created a fiscal, social, military, and ecological disaster. "If all you have is a hammer, every problem looks like a nail."

40,000 Americans die on roads every year. Traffic costs $2.76 trillion. American roads earn a D+ maintenance grade. 1.2% of US land is walkable — yet produces 20% of GDP.

The solution exists. It was identified by Congress in 1975. It's been running injury-free in Morgantown, WV since 1975 — over 100 million passenger-miles. JPods is solar-powered, grade-separated, 10x lower cost than highways, 3,000x safer than roads.

The barrier isn't technical. It's institutional. Massachusetts broke it in 2021 — funded $1.3M unanimously, Governor signed. The 5X5 Standard is law. Now we need makers to build it, city by city.

"In 1975, Congress identified podcar networks as the solution. They also noted that government 'institutional failures' had blocked such innovations for 'four to six decades.' That was 50 years ago. The barrier is over a century old. We're breaking it."

References

Books — The Intellectual Foundation

  • Jane Jacobs — The Death and Life of Great American Cities (1961); Cities and the Wealth of Nations (1984); The Economy of Cities (1969). The original case for walkable, mixed-use, bottom-up urbanism.
  • Donald Shoup — The High Cost of Free Parking (2005). Cities spend more on free parking than schools. Parking minimums destroy the tax base.
  • Robert Bullard — Highway Robbery: Transportation Racism & New Routes to Equity (2004). Federal highway construction replaced Jim Crow with concrete — same communities destroyed, different tool.
  • Jeff Speck — Walkable City (2012); Walkable City Rules (2018). The practical guide to making cities walkable. 10 steps, all measurable.
  • Megan Kimble — City Limits: Infrastructure, Inequality, and the Future of America's Highways (2024). The current fight — freeway fighters, induced demand, who benefits and who pays.
  • Chuck Marohn — Strong Towns: A Bottom-Up Revolution to Rebuild American Prosperity (2019); Confessions of a Recovering Engineer (2021). Cities are insolvent. The smallest next step wins.
  • Bill James — Climate Change Root Cause. How Federal mercantile monopolies in transport and energy created the crisis. ClimateChangeRootCause.com
