# Humans Are Tactile

**Source:** Bill James, 2026-07-17
**Applies to:** JPods and WC3 — all domains, all outputs

---

> *"Humans are tactile. We must make their touch feel balanced and sturdy."*

---

## The Principle

Every point where the system meets a human body — a printed page held
in the hand, a pod seat under the passenger, a station platform under
the foot, a screen under the finger — must feel balanced and sturdy.

This is not a design preference. It is a trust requirement. Humans
judge competence through touch before they judge it through thought.
A flimsy invoice, a wobbly pod, a cluttered screen — each says
"the people who built this didn't care enough" before the conscious
mind even processes the content.

---

## In WC3

The invoice is the handshake between the business and the customer.

| Tactile quality | What it means in print |
|----------------|----------------------|
| **Balanced** | Visual weight distributed. Eye flows: company → customer → lines → totals. Nothing crowds. Nothing floats unmoored. |
| **Sturdy** | Borders consistent. Columns align. Numbers right-justify. Footer doesn't collide with last line item. Looks designed, not generated. |
| **Clean** | No orphan reports cluttering the list. No "Proposal 2" with no explanation. Every form that exists earned its place. |
| **Readable** | Font size respects human eyes. Default M (18px), not S (12px). The program bends to the person, not the reverse. |

The form IS the business at the moment of payment. If the customer
holds an invoice that feels flimsy, she questions the business —
not the software.

## In JPods

The pod is the handshake between the network and the passenger.

| Tactile quality | What it means physically |
|----------------|------------------------|
| **Balanced** | Pod doesn't lean. Acceleration is smooth. Deceleration doesn't lurch. Weight distribution is centered. |
| **Sturdy** | Door closes firmly. Seat doesn't flex. Handhold doesn't give. The passenger feels contained, not trapped. |
| **Clean** | Station platform is uncluttered. Signage is clear. No exposed wiring, no peeling labels, no ambiguous buttons. |
| **Quiet** | Motor hum, not motor whine. Wind noise managed. No rattles. Silence communicates precision. |

The passenger judges the entire network — the engineering, the safety,
the competence of the organization — in the first three seconds of
sitting down. Before the pod moves.

## Across All Domains

| Domain | The tactile moment | What balanced and sturdy means |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------------------|
| Printed invoice | Customer holds the page | Aligned columns, crisp logo, totals where expected |
| DataBrowser | User scans the list | Readable font, clear headers, no clutter |
| PDF Designer | User drags a field | Snap to grid, smooth movement, immediate render |
| JPods pod | Passenger sits down | Firm seat, smooth door, no vibration |
| JPods station | Passenger steps on platform | Level surface, clear markings, intuitive flow |
| JPods guideway | Passenger looks out | Smooth travel, no jolt at switches, consistent speed |
| Alice coaching | User reads the tip | Clear language, actionable, no jargon |
| Barn cleaner | User reviews a report | Real data rendered, clear choices, immediate feedback |

## The Standard

If a human touches it and feels uncertainty — the column is off by
two pixels, the pod hesitates at the switch, the font is too small
to read without squinting — we failed. Not partially. Completely.

Balanced and sturdy is not a polish pass. It is the product.
Everything else is infrastructure.

## Alice Reads Confusion Before the User Speaks It

> *"Not wait for the person to say they are confused, but ask if
> she can assist."*

Humans telegraph confusion through behavior before they articulate it:

| Behavior | What It Means | Alice's Response |
|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Opened same report 3x in an hour | Can't find what she needs | "I noticed you opened this report three times. Can I help you find what you're looking for?" |
| 8 minutes on a page without action | Stuck, doesn't know next step | "You've been on this proposal a while. Would you like me to show you the print options?" |
| Zoomed in twice | Font too small, can't read | "Want me to increase your default font size?" |
| Printed, then reprinted | First output didn't look right | "The last print didn't look right? I can show you other layouts for this invoice." |
| Hovered over 4 reports without clicking | Can't tell them apart | "These report names are confusing. Want me to show you a preview of each one?" |
| Searched, got results, searched again | First results weren't what she expected | "Your search for 'bat' returned 40 items. Were you looking for a specific product?" |
| Clicked Back 3 times | Lost in navigation | "Looks like you're trying to get back to the order. Here's a shortcut." |

**This is not surveillance. This is a good shopkeeper.**

The shopkeeper sees the customer looking at two products, picking one
up, putting it down, picking up the other. She walks over and says
"Can I help you choose?" The customer can say "I'm fine" and the
shopkeeper backs off. No data collected. No profile built. Just
human attention, digitally expressed.

**The sovereignty rule applies:** Alice asks, she doesn't act. She
offers, she doesn't push. She notices, she doesn't record (unless
the user opts into coaching analytics via Collaborate_WebClerk).

**Look → Touch → Decide.** The user looks at the screen (visual).
She touches the mouse, hesitates, moves it, hesitates again (tactile
behavior). She hasn't decided yet — that hesitation is the moment
Alice can help. After the decision, it's too late. The help needed
to come before the confusion became frustration.
