## Launch Sequence (first 10 posts)

### Post 1: "Luck Is When Preparation Meets Opportunity"
The elevator pitch. Three forces converging. 28 years of preparation. Horizontal Elevators. Links to CityTool and MeshMobility.
*End with: Type your city into CityTool. See what happens.*

### Post 2: "The Prime Law of Networks"
V = n²/p. Why packet size determines network value. Why JPods is packet-switching for physical movement. The Internet analogy — precise, not metaphorical.
*End with: BetterPhysics.com*

### Post 3: "Tricia Nixon Said It Was More Fun Than Disneyland"
Morgantown PRT — 1972 to present. 100 million injury-free passenger-miles. 16,000 riders/day. The technology that works and nobody knows about.
*End with: You can ride SkyRide today in Waconia, MN.*

### Post 4: "A Bureaucrat Killed It With Two Sentences"
The Secaucus story. Unanimous town council. NJDOT's two-sentence dismissal. The NJ Legislature had already directed NJDOT to study PRT. The Transportation Chairman's framework was ignored by his own DOT.
*End with: Massachusetts solved it. Here's how.*

### Post 5: "Massachusetts Broke the Barrier"
S.2309 → H5248. Unanimous. $1.3M funded. Governor signed. The 5X5 Standard in law. ASTM F24. What this means for every other state.
*End with: The regulatory barrier is broken. Now what?*

### Post 6: "Steve Jobs Loved It But Said No"
The 1999 Apple tours. WebClerk. Jobs and the Physical Internet. "Regulatory barriers too severe." Segway instead. iPods 18 months later. Jobs was right about the barrier. Massachusetts broke it 22 years later.
*End with: What would Jobs think now that Glydways raised $170M?*

### Post 7: "40,000 Americans Die on Roads Every Year"
The safety argument. ASTM F24 — 3,000x safer. Mike Teske — 110 Disney/Universal rides certified. The difference between ASTM accountability and ATN sovereign immunity. Government and liberty, each in their proper role.
*End with: Design a safer network for your city. It takes 5 minutes.*

### Post 8: "1.2% of Land Produces 20% of GDP"
The walkability/prosperity argument. Shoup, Jane Jacobs, StrongTowns, Urban3. Arlington VA: 10% walkable = 50% GDP. Car infrastructure destroys the tax base it depends on. JPods converts parking lots to productive land.
*End with: Run CityTool for your city. See the fiscal impact.*

### Post 9: "Want a Sustainable Future? Create It"
The student kit program. 10xMakers. Design → Survey → Build → Ride. $50 cargo robot. $300 rideable vehicle. Every kit is a sensor. Every student is a surveyor. Pirates and Patriots.
*End with: 10xMakers.com*

### Post 10: "The $240K That Starts Everything"
The F&F pitch. Mall of America. SkyRide in Waconia. "We Win, You Win" vendor agreements. 2x equity bonus, 3x conversion option. The cascade: $240K → $6M → $20M → $600M.
*End with: If you want in, here's how. [link to F&F page]*
