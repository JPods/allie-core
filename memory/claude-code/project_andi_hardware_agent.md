Andi is not just server software — Andi is the box plus the intelligence. When a business adopts the system, they receive physical hardware (Mac Mini, GEEKOM IT15, etc.) with Andi pre-installed as their local AI agent, running WC3, Alice, MeshMobility, MyCarryOn, and JPods robot interfaces on their own machine.

**Why:** This is the Desktop Hosting model from Bill's 2002 Wiley book, realized with modern hardware and AI. No SaaS, no central server holding everyone's data. Each business owns their hardware, their data, their agent. Same sovereignty principle as JPods — locally owned, locally governed.

**How to apply:**
- Andi is the product that ships with the hardware — not a cloud service
- Each Andi instance is sovereign — data stays on the owner's machine
- Opt-in sharing with Allie creates a voluntary intelligence network (bottom-up, not centralized)
- Allie gets patterns, not raw data (same fading principle as MyCarryOn)
- The sharing is enumerated and revocable — CarryOn model applied to AI experience
- This is the Physical Internet applied to computing: distributed, locally owned, packet-switched intelligence
