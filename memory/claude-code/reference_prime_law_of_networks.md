Bill owns the PrimeLawOfNetworks domains. His formulation:

> "Network value increases exponentially as the number of interconnected nodes increases and the packet-size is driven towards one."

**The formula:** V ∝ n² / p
- n = interconnected nodes
- p = packet size (mass per unit moved)
- As p → 1, full Metcalfe value unlocked (on-demand, every O-D pair served)
- As p → ∞, value collapses (batch networks: buses at 20 tons, trains at 100 tons)

**Why this matters:** Metcalfe's Law applies to information networks where packets are free to copy. Physical networks have a constraint: the packet has mass. Highways move 2-ton packets. Buses batch 40 people into 20-ton packets. The larger the packet, the more you need schedules, fixed routes, and batching — which means most origin-destination pairs can't be served directly.

JPods moves 500kg packets on-demand. This is circuit-switching vs packet-switching applied to physical movement. The internet won by routing small packets dynamically instead of dedicating circuits. JPods does the same to transit.

**Connection to the ecosystem:**
- JPods as "WiFi of the Physical Internet" (Middle Mile)
- Cargo/waste streaming (continuous flow, packet size = one package, not one truck)
- Fresh waste sorting (small packets = recoverable; large batch = landfill)
- The 5X5 Standard: privately funded, 5x more efficient, 5% gross revenue royalty
