CityTool calculates a shorter payback than reality. Bill manually sets 7-year payback for capital presentations.

**Why:** One-time savings like reduced car ownership take years to unwind. People don't sell their cars on day one. Mode shift is gradual — 30% over several years, not overnight. Presenting an unrealistically short payback undermines credibility with capital sources.

**How to apply:** When presenting economics to investors or cities, use 7-year payback minimum. The math may show 2-3 years but the behavioral transition is slower. Conservative estimates build trust; aggressive ones invite skepticism.
