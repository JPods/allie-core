Small-Stings are a customer accountability mechanism in the JPods commercial model.

Two-way financial flow:
- **Customer → JPods:** Customer assesses a fine (a "Small-Sting") for a problem they believe should have been solved. This is a formal complaint with a financial consequence.
- **JPods → Customer:** JPods pays customers to provide retrospections (structured feedback). The payment creates an incentive for customers to document what went wrong and what should improve.

**Why:** Aligns incentives — JPods is motivated to resolve problems to avoid fines; customers are motivated to provide useful feedback because they are paid for it. Retrospection-as-paid-service is a bottom-up quality loop, not top-down auditing.

**Who owns it:** Alice accounts for both flows — incoming Small-Sting fines and outgoing retrospection payments — as part of her ticket sales and action list responsibilities in WC3.

**How to apply:** When designing Alice's commercial accounting or WC3 transaction records, include Small-Stings as a transaction type alongside ticket sales. Retrospection payments are also Alice's domain.
