## What the followus ribbon exposed

The yellow ribbon makes path data visible to the eye for the first time.
Two problems are now unambiguous:

### 1. gw_uturn tracks on inside rail, not centerline
- Ribbon traces the 3m diameter (1500mm radius) inside rail arc
- Vehicle should travel the 3.5m diameter (1750mm radius) centerline
- Root cause: ArcCurves in SketchUp station templates ARE the inside rail
  (MIN_STATION_ARC_RADIUS_MM = 1500mm enforces the inside rail, not the centerline)
- path.json extraction copies these inside-rail pts directly into the vehicle lookup
- Fix: after extracting ArcCurve pts, detect radius ≈ 1500mm and scale outward
  by BEAM_WIDTH/2 (250mm) to the centerline — both in the extracted.json path
  (Code Path 1, jpod_path_json.rb ~line 265) and in _generate_uturn_arc_pts_mm
  (Code Path 2, jpod_path_json.rb ~line 860)

### 2. Inter-station curved guideways drawn as straight chords
- Ribbon shows straight line from CP to CP even when the guideway curves in XY
- Root cause: anim_lookup (path.json + map.json) only stores 2-pt chords for
  seg_* inter-station segments — no Bezier pts
- The vehicle animator gets the curve by stitching structure_followme_paths +
  edge_lookup; the followus only uses anim_lookup, missing both sources
- Fix: in show_route_followus_overlay, for 2-pt seg_ entries, call
  build_edge_lookup_from_model (or equivalent) to upgrade chords to arc pts
  the same way the animator does for vehicle paths

## Why this matters
The vehicle "squiggle dance" on gw_uturn is now explained:
the vehicle is following the inside rail (1500mm), not the centerline (1750mm).
The inside rail is the structural edge of the beam, not the travel lane.
