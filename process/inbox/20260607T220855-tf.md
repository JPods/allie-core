summary: |
  Nora should record junction jerks as physical.json defects and ask Noelle
  to fix the map coordinate that caused them.

  A "jerk" at a track boundary (seg_↔gw_ junction) is a symptom of Z or
  direction mismatch in the map. The SketchUp Show Route junction Z check
  already detects this geometrically. Nora detects it physically (IMU spike
  or sudden heading change). Both are observing the same defect from different
  vantage points.

  Design:
    1. Nora detects a jerk (IMU angular velocity spike or encoder step anomaly
       at a known segment boundary)
    2. Nora writes to physical.json:
         type: "jerk"
         location_t: (fraction along segment where jerk occurred)
         severity: minor | moderate | severe
         description: "seg_↔gw_ boundary jerk at S001.CP0"
         logged_at: UTC
         logged_by: NORA_xxxx
    3. Nora notifies Noelle (MQTT or fault file):
         "physical defect at junction — map review requested"
    4. Noelle re-validates the junction coordinate:
         - Re-run CP-math for that connection point
         - Check extracted.json vs. world transform for the affected station
         - If mismatch > Z_JUNCTION_TOL_MM: flag SEVERE, block that route until fixed

  The jerk IS the Z mismatch manifesting physically. Fixing the map (correct CP
  world transform, correct extracted.json) makes the jerk disappear. No smoothing
  in firmware — fix the source, not the symptom.
