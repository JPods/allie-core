principle: Platform placement authority lives in two separate layers that can drift:
           (1) platform hash (from followme export) carries start_m/end_m and track_index;
           (2) map.json carries actual gw_platform pts and successor topology.
           When these drift apart, always prefer map.json — it is generated fresh
           by Noelle on every Build and carries the physical truth.
           The synthetic host guideway must store its own track_index (1 = entrance
           at pts[0]) so Tier 2 slot math doesn't depend on the stale platform hash.

definitions_clarified:
  platform_host guideway: Synthetic JPods Guideway group created by
    ensure_platform_host_guideway. Its beam_path must follow gw_platform pts
    (from map.json), not start_m/end_m (which are CP stub endpoints).
    track_index is always 1 when built from map.json gw_platform:
    entrance=pts[0] (inbound/red end), exit=pts.last (outbound/blue end).

  track_index: 1 = entrance at pts[0], vehicles travel path direction (pts[0]→pts.last).
               0 = entrance at pts.last, vehicles travel reverse of path direction.
               gw_platform always uses track_index=1: inbound end is pts[0].
