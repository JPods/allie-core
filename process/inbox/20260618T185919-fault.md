## DEFECT 1 — N_N arc interior pts on outside edge (all four arcs)

Bill: "gw_c_0_0, gw_c_1_1, gw_c_2_2, and gw_c_3_3 have their centerlines on the outside edge,
250mm from the centerline of the 15m circle."

Each N_N arc has 13 pts. Measured distance of every pt from circle center [-15881.728, 0]:

| Track    | pt[0]    | pt[1..11]  | pt[12]   |
|----------|----------|------------|----------|
| gw_c_0_0 | 7766mm ✗ | 7766mm ✗   | 7516mm ✓ |
| gw_c_1_1 | 7516mm ✓ | 7766mm ✗   | 7516mm ✓ |
| gw_c_2_2 | 7516mm ✓ | 7766mm ✗   | 7516mm ✓ |
| gw_c_3_3 | 7516mm ✓ | 7766mm ✗   | 7516mm ✓ |

Interior pts (pts[1..11] for all; pt[0] for gw_c_0_0) are on the OUTSIDE EDGE at 7766mm.
Only the connection endpoints (7516mm pts) are on the centerline.

Outside edge − centerline = 7766 − 7516 = 250mm. Matches BEAM_WIDTH/2.

Consequence: N_N arc shapes bow outward 250mm. Vehicle path incorrect for 11 of 13 pts.
Connector endpoint correctness is incidental — they happen to be at the centerline because
they were extracted from the SketchUp entity at the rail junction, which IS at centerline
where the beam meets the connector.

Root cause: Priority B centroid-arc synthesis in populate_from_open_template uses the
raw 2-pt chord endpoints from extracted.json to fit the arc. Those endpoints are at
centerline (7516mm) but the arc is swept at the wrong radius — Priority B calls
_arc_pts_from_center_mm which samples the arc at the wrong radius from the circle center.
The fitted radius is 7766mm (outside edge) instead of 7516mm (centerline).
