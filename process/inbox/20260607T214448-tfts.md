  - try:      beam_path_fallback — scan every 'JPods Guideway' group in model, read beam_path
              attribute, subtract BEAM_DEPTH to get vehicle-level Z
    result:   failed — wrong architecture
    revealed: (1) Three sources of truth existed simultaneously: beam_path attribute,
              followme.json tracks[], and CP cache. Each gave slightly different Z.
              (2) beam_path Z is beam TOP; subtract BEAM_DEPTH = vehicle level = cp[:center].z_mm.
              So the fallback was computing: (beam_top - BEAM_DEPTH) × 25.4 = cp[:center].z × 25.4.
              The math was hiding in the fallback. The datum was already CP center all along.
              (3) cached_connection_point returned definition-local coords without applying
              entity.transformation — correct on flat terrain (Z≈0), wrong on non-flat terrain.

  - try:      Z-snap — after generating map.json, snap gw_cp_in / gw_cp_out outer endpoints
              to match adjacent seg_ Z
    result:   failed — wrong architecture
    revealed: Snapping is admission that two sources disagree. One source of truth means
              the math computes the junction Z once and all consumers read from it.
              Also found: Z-snap was targeting gw_cp_in FIRST pt (inner end = 7686.8mm)
              instead of LAST pt (outer end = 7936.8mm), producing a spurious 270mm delta.

  - try:      CP-math + world transformation — replace both fallback and snap with:
              StructurePlacer.connection_point(entity, index) for map.json seg_ endpoints;
              entity.transformation applied in cached_connection_point for Build pipeline
    result:   succeeded
    revealed: StructurePlacer.connection_point already applies entity.transformation correctly.
              CP center world position defines the seg_ junction exactly. When terrain changes
              and the station instance moves, transformation updates and Bezier follows.

principle: |
  One source of truth. Do the math.

  CP connections directly connect CP world coordinates. The from_cp[:center] and to_cp[:center]
  world positions — obtained by applying the station instance's world transformation to the
  definition-local cp_marker hub vertex — define the seg_ endpoints exactly and completely.

  Do not scan 3D geometry to recover what the math already knows. Do not snap data to cover
  a mismatch between two sources. The math is: entity.transformation × definition_local_cp_center
  = world_cp_position = seg_ endpoint.

  When terrain changes, entity.transformation changes. The Bezier adapts. No rebuild of the
  CP cache, no re-scan, no snap. One formula, one time, one result everywhere.

  Corollary — reject if the math cannot run: if the station entity or its CP is not found,
  log and skip that seg_. No fallback to approximate geometry. The map is only as accurate
  as the model.
