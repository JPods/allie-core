summary: Ring junction endpoints for short connectors and in/out approach tracks must
         come from the RING ARC's own FIRST/LAST pts — never from switch-box geometry.

code:    templates/track_formations/traffic_circle7/geometry.json — all gw_c_X_Y and gw_in/gw_out tracks

detail:  The traffic_circle7 ring has four semicircular arcs (gw_c_0_0, gw_c_1_1,
         gw_c_2_2, gw_c_3_3) whose FIRST/LAST pts define the exact ring-centerline
         positions at each junction EP.

         The four short connectors (gw_c_0_1, gw_c_1_2, gw_c_2_3, gw_c_3_0) and the
         eight approach tracks (gw_in/gw_out) all had endpoints ~500mm INSIDE the ring
         radius — at switch-box offset positions, not on the ring centerline.

         Effect: the short connector ribbons visually crossed the guideway (one end
         on-ring, one end off-ring). The approach tracks drew to the wrong point.

         Fix: every connector/approach endpoint that touches a ring junction must
         equal the adjacent arc's FIRST or LAST point exactly.

         Rule: When gw_c_A_B connects arc A.LAST to arc B.FIRST (or B.LAST for
         reversed arcs), set:
           gw_c_A_B.FIRST = arc_A.LAST  (or arc_A.FIRST if vehicle reverses)
           gw_c_A_B.LAST  = arc_B entry point

         For in/out approach tracks: ring-side endpoint = the EP coordinate (same
         point used by the adjacent short connector).

apply_to: Any ring-topology template. If you see ribbons "crossing" at a junction,
          check whether the endpoint is on the ring arc (correct radius from ring
          center) or has drifted to an off-arc offset.

domain:  SU
