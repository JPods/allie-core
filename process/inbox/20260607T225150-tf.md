summary: |
  Sally owns station route compliance and parking enforcement.

  Sally is not just a slot registry — she is the authority that ensures every
  pod passing through her station follows the declared chain sequence and that
  parking rules are honored. She has two enforcement duties:

  1. ROUTE COMPLIANCE
     Every pod entering a station must follow the gw_chain sequence declared
     in lines.json for its assigned route. Sally checks that the pod traverses
     the correct sequence of gw_* tracks in order. If a pod skips a required
     track or takes the wrong branch (e.g., exits via parking when routed
     through), Sally flags it as a route violation and holds the pod.

     Implementation: Sally reads the chain for the pod's current route segment
     from lines.json. As each gw_track is entered, Sally ticks it off the
     expected sequence. Out-of-order or missing track = hold + fault.

  2. PARKING ENFORCEMENT
     A pod may park ONLY in a designated parking slot AND ONLY when:
       a. Its route is complete (destination reached), OR
       b. It has been explicitly dispatched to park by Natalie

     Sally checks: if a pod enters a hold_loop or parking track without a
     park authorization from Natalie, it is a rogue park — Sally ejects it
     back into the through-flow and logs a fault.

     Implementation: Sally maintains a parking_queue with authorized pod IDs.
     Any pod entering a parking gw_* not on that list is an unauthorized park.

  Both duties apply to the physical model (Pi Sally chip) and the SketchUp
  simulation (jpod_sally.rb proof run). The lines.json chain declarations are
  the authority — Sally reads them, not re-derives them.
