    result:   worked but required two builds — unacceptable for users
    revealed: the root cause is that `cp_center.z + BEAM_DEPTH` fails when
              entity.transformation has lateral slope (Y→Z coupling); the formula

  - try:      Single-pass: read gw_cp_out/in outer tip from geometry.json
              (tracks section, last pt of each gw_cp track = outer/gate end),

    result:   succeeded — anchor Z is exact on first build; Z_CONTINUITY faults
              clear without a second build; no map.json dependency
    revealed: geometry.json tracks are in definition-local space (same coordinate
              frame that entity.transformation maps to world space);
              station_thru_dip CP0: gw_cp_out_0 at Y=1250.9mm, gw_cp_in_0 at
              Y=4750.9mm — 3500mm apart (DUAL_TRACK_SPACING), which is why the
              cp_center (midpoint) formula fails by hundreds of mm on sloped terrain

principle: The CP center is the midpoint between the two rails. Any formula that
           starts from cp_center cannot correctly compute either rail's world Z
           when entity.transformation has lateral slope. The geometry.json outer
           tip point is already in definition-local space — applying
           entity.transformation is the only math needed. This is what
           "entity.transformation IS the terrain adaptation" means (Axiom 19).
           geo_cache (keyed by formation_id) is shared across a build so each
           template's geometry.json is parsed once regardless of how many segments
           connect to stations of that type.
