summary: |
  Z bounce at gw_cp_in_↔lead and seg_↔gw_cp_in_ junctions — source and fix.

  SOURCE: Two compounding causes, both in frame-of-reference (Axiom 18).

  1. BEZIER TANGENT Z (primary — gw_cp_in_ interior bounce)
     ene_railroad controls[2/3] are 3D tangent vectors (magnitude ≈ chord/3 in
     SketchUp inches). If the track has any Z tilt in the model, these vectors
     carry a non-zero Z component. _bezier_pts_from_tangents_mm converts them to
     mm (×25.4) and adds to the endpoint Z as the Bezier handle offset.
     Result: b1[2] = sp_z + sv_z × 25.4, b2[2] = ep_z + ev_z × 25.4 — the
     Bezier interior rises/falls immediately after the start pt and returns to
     ep_z at the end. This creates a sub-3m Z reversal right at the junction.

  2. OUTSIDE-RAIL ARC RADIUS (uturn at 4m instead of 3.5m)
     _generate_uturn_arc_pts_mm only snapped r to centerline (1750mm) if r ≈ 1500mm
     (inside rail). When extracted endpoints are on the OUTSIDE RAIL (r ≈ 2000mm),
     the guard failed → arc generated at 4m diameter instead of 3.5m centerline.
     Same blind spot in the export-path arc centerline correction.

  FIXES:
  A. populate_from_open_template: zero Z of sv and ev before passing to
     _bezier_pts_from_tangents_mm. Station-interior tracks are horizontal —
     any tangent Z is a modeling artifact. Zeroing here fixes future Extract Template runs.

  B. Network export path: for gw_cp_in_N tracks with Z span < Z_ARC_TILT_MAX_MM,
     linearize Z from first to last pt across all interior pts. Removes the Bezier
     Z bow from stale extracted.json without requiring re-extraction.

  C. _generate_uturn_arc_pts_mm: snap r to centerline (1750mm) when r ≈ inside rail
     (1500mm) OR r ≈ outside rail (2000mm). Tolerance ±150mm covers extraction variance.
     Same fix applied to the arc centerline correction in the export path.

  D. Step 9 added to PathJSON.export: scans all tracks for any X/Y/Z component
     that reverses within 3000mm of travel. Reports as [BOUNCE] — does not block
     export. This is the automated form of Nora's path quality rule for the SketchUp
     proof side.

  DETECTION: sub-3m reversal = [BOUNCE] in Ruby console after Build.

  flagged templates if still present (the Z linearization should clear gw_cp_in_ bounces
  automatically; remaining bounces need model geometry fix).
