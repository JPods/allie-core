principle: When a first pt is snapped to an adjacent track's endpoint, any intermediate
           pts built relative to the ORIGINAL first pt are no longer on the new sp→ep line.
           simplify_collinear (even at 1mm) will then collapse them to at most one
           surviving "bend" pt. Two defenses are needed:
           (A) re-sample after snap so all pts lie on the new line, AND
           (B) skip simplify_collinear for uniformly-sampled tracks.
           Either alone is insufficient: (A) without (B) → re-sampled pts are again
           perfectly collinear → removed by simplify. (B) without (A) → pts are on the
           old line, creating a visible kink at the snap joint.

secondary: The 'sampled' flag is the right signal. It marks tracks where pts were
           placed by arithmetic interpolation at a fixed interval — as opposed to
           arc-generation (math arc) or ArcCurve extraction (Bezier). simplify_collinear
           is only safe for scan-based or ArcCurve-derived pts where redundant pts are
           accidental, not for density pts that are deliberate.
