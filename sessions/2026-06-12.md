## Lessons for Allie

1. **Remote branch is the reference, not local drift** — When 29 commits of Z fixes all failed
   and Bill pointed to the remote, the correct architecture was already there. Before the next
   Z_CONTINUITY-class problem: check if origin/su_jpods_claude already has the solution.
   [SketchUp]

2. **Stubs removed = references to stubs removed** — The codebase made a deliberate decision
   to remove stubs ~2 weeks ago. Any fix that reintroduces geometry.json stub reading is
   going backward. When a concept has been removed from the codebase, do not reintroduce it
   even if it looks like the shortest path to the fix. Ask why it was removed first.
   [SketchUp] [Cross-domain]

3. **Synthesis beats reading when the datum is already available** — gw_cp outer tip Z is not
   a fact stored somewhere — it is derived. When both the gw_cp and the seg_ are derived from
   cp_center.z, they match trivially. The 187.5mm discrepancy was real in geometry.json but
   irrelevant to the synthesis approach. Find what both sides agree is the canonical datum, then
   derive both from it. [SketchUp]

4. **Two wrong turns before the right one costs time but not progress** — The TFTS arcs are the
   valuable artifact. The 20260612T172000 TFTS captures the principle that makes the right answer
   obvious in retrospect. The prior TFTS captures the path. Both are needed.
   [Cross-domain]
