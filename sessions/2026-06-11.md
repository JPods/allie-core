### Bug 5 — hold loop return ps4→ps1→ps4 (fixed this session)
**Root cause 1**: landing chains for station_thru_dip included `gw_platform` as the last track. The pod traversed gw_platform from entry [6765,...] to exit [-4515,...] — past ps4 [-1985,...] — before park_dispatch fired. No forward-only park clip was possible from exit end.

**Root cause 2**: `pod.entity.bounds.center` used as seed_pos for park maneuver clipping. In 3D station geometry, bounds.center was [-5015,-8042,7094]mm — 7000mm off the platform in Y/Z. Projection d_proj >> FOLLOWME_CONNECT_TOL_RELAXED (3m) → @t=0 → park maneuver started from entry end, not pod's arrival position.

**Fix A**: Removed `gw_platform` from both landing chains (`in_cp0`, `in_cp1`) in `station_thru_dip/lines.json`. Chains now end at `gw_platform_parking` — same pattern as station_line_end. Pod stops at gw_platform_parking exit = gw_platform entry end. Park dispatch drives gw_platform to assigned slot.

**Fix B**: Replaced `pod.entity.bounds.center` with `last_man&.dig(:pts)&.last` as seed_pos in the park maneuver arrival handler (jpod_vehicle_anim.rb lines ~2798 and ~2821). `last_man[:pts].last` is the exact point the animation system placed the pod at — no lag, no geometry offset.

## Principles confirmed

1. **Landing chains end at gw_platform_parking, not gw_platform.** gw_platform is the parking track — it belongs to the park maneuver. Including it in the landing chain overshoots the slot. Universal rule for all station templates.

2. **entity.bounds.center is not a reliable track position.** Use `last_man[:pts].last` (the animation system's exact endpoint). bounds.center lags one tick and includes pod body geometry offset — unreliable in 3D stations.
